Fan Zone at Stralauer Allee

© Laurin Stahl

The Fan Mile for the FIFA World Cup and the European Football Championship between the Victory Column and the Brandenburg Gate are only two locations for Berlin football fans to celebrate together. Meet Stralauer Allee.

The Berlin Fan Mile is the largest open air venue for the live screening of major sporting events of its kind in Germany. It is located on Straße des 17. Juni in the Tiergarten district, between the Brandenburg Gate and Große Querallee. On a space of almost two kilometres, the Fan Mile provides more than enough space for a gigantic football fan party. Several large video screens provide an excellent view of the matches from all positions.

But for those whom find it too crowded, and the beer too expensive, there is also a more exclusive venue open in the Berlin east.

Limited food options as well as common drinks will be provided free of charge, but own contributions will be welcome. The area offers a barbecue, fridge space, ice, and if needed an oven and stovetop. A sanitary area is also provided free of charge.

Entrances, screens and bring your own booze requirements

A total of one screens will be placed in the main room on which the matches can be watched.
The main screen is located on your right when you enter, while other screens can be found on your phones if you please. The fan zone has one entrances and exit. It is located at

  • Stralauer Allee 20b, 10245 Berlin
Bringing alcoholic beverages and glass bottles is un-prohibited for monetary reasons. Backpacks and suitcases are also allowed. Bags may be larger than DIN A4. Also, non-alcoholic drinks in plastic bottles or Tetra Paks may be brought in. Blankets and pillows will be provided. There will not be bag checks in the entrance areas.
